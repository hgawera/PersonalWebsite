My Project

Extended Project Qualififcation

During my A-Levels we had the opportunity to write a detailed report about a topic of our choice. With no/little knowledge into AI at the time, yet with a hungry desire to learn, my EPQ was titled "Can Ethics and Morality be implemented and exploited fully in AI?". Above is an image of the IBM DeepBlue AI project, and below is the then chess world chamption Gary Kasparov facing 15 chess computers simultaneously. All extensively talked in my project. To read, click the image below. The Synopsis is as followed:

As society becomes more and more integrated with technology, we start to demand more from machines. We incorporate them into our everyday lives, preforming complex tasks. Artificial Intelligence systems in particular have advanced a great deal in the last decade. South Korea, in conjunction with Samsung, are creating autonomous weapons as a protocol to defend its border with North Korea. Recently we saw examples of driverless cars on our roads, fully autonomous, with no driver needed. With systems like these, we ask ourselves imperative questions needed to design and fulfil the needs of such a system, the main focus of course is; Does AI need morals? With the power we give AI, it puts the system in charge of making a life or death decision in some cases.

My project talks about why AI need a moral and ethical code. From whose/what morality, to examples of attempts already made. It also discusses how engineers, scientist and ethicists go about designing such a conglomerate task, exploring different software options to hardware options. Moreover, I discuss the dangers of moral AI and the caution needed to design such a system. Furthermore, I explore the effects on the individual, and the human race.
